# Video Script: The "so what" move

You've done your research. You've synthesized your findings. Now comes the question that separates a good report from a great one: so what?

This is the move that turns research into strategy. It's where you stop describing the landscape and start saying what it means for your project. What should your team do differently because of what you found? What constraints does the research reveal? What assumptions are you making, and why?

In professional work, this is the moment clients are actually paying for. The research is the foundation, but the strategic point of view is the value. It's where you demonstrate that you didn't just gather information. You thought about it.

The hardest part is being specific. "Our research suggests we should take a targeted approach" says nothing. "Our research shows that the organization's primary audience doesn't use the channel the brief assumes, which means we need to propose an alternative" says everything. Be concrete. Name what you found and connect it directly to what you recommend.
