# Video Script: From "what we found" to "what it means"

There's a pattern that shows up in almost every junior analyst's first report. They do good research. They find useful information. And then they present it as a list of things they found.

That's a summary. It's not analysis.

The shift you need to make this week is from reporting what you found to explaining what it means. When a senior consultant reads a research section, they're not looking for a catalog of facts. They're looking for patterns, connections, and the conclusions you drew from them.

Here's the test: if someone else did completely different research on the same topic, would your research section still say the same thing? If yes, you've written a summary. If your section could only have been written by someone who did your specific research and thought carefully about what it revealed, that's synthesis.

That's what this section of your deliverable is asking for.
