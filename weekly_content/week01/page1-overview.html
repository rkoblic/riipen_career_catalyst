Getting Started

Welcome to Career Catalyst. This first week is about getting oriented: understanding how this course works, what's expected of you, and what you'll be working toward. There's no team yet and no project, just the groundwork that will make everything else click once both arrive.

Video Transcript: Why this course is different

This course doesn't work like most of your other classes. There are no exams. No lectures to sit through. Instead, you'll work on a real project for a real organization, as part of a team.

That might sound exciting, or it might sound a little intimidating. Either way, it's worth understanding why the course is built this way.

The professional world runs on projects. Teams form, figure out a problem together, deliver something, and move on to the next one. The skills that matter most in that context, communication, collaboration, critical thinking, those don't develop from reading about them. They develop from doing them, reflecting on what happened, and trying again.

That's what experiential learning is. You learn by doing real work, not simulated work. And because the project is real, the stakes feel different. Your employer contact is a real person. Your deliverables will be used. The feedback you get is professional feedback.

This week is your on-ramp. No team yet, no project yet. Just the foundations you'll need once both arrive. Take your time with it. Everything you encounter this week will show up again later in the course.

What's Happening This Week

This week you'll work through a series of short modules that introduce the foundations of the course: how experiential learning works, how you'll use AI, how you'll be assessed, and what professional skills you'll start building from day one. You'll also browse the available project briefs on the Riipen platform (we'll walk you through how to access them later in the week) and submit your preferences for project matching.

What's due

  • Your Starting Point Questionnaire (completion-based). An honest self-assessment plus your ranked project preferences with written rationale. You'll reference back to this throughout the course as you reflect on your growth. Must be completed without AI assistance (though you can use AI to help you compare briefs before you write your rationale).

Suggested order of completion

  1. Start here, then work through the foundational modules in order: experiential learning, syllabus review, and the AI policy module.
  2. Browse the project briefs and complete the Your Starting Point Questionnaire (which includes your project preferences).
  3. Finish with the professional skill modules (reflective practice, time management, professional email) and the preview of what's coming next.

There's no single right order after the first few modules, but completing the foundational content before browsing project briefs will help you evaluate your options against meaningful criteria (skill development, career fit, growth goals) rather than just going with whichever brief sounds interesting first.

Introduce Yourself

Before teams are assigned, take a few minutes to introduce yourself to the course community. Post a brief introduction in the discussion forum: who you are, what you're studying, and what drew you to this course. Then read through a few of your peers' posts and respond to two or three that catch your attention.

This isn't busywork. Building social presence early, even something as simple as a short post and a few replies, is one of the strongest predictors of how well teams collaborate later. The people posting alongside you are your future teammates. Knowing a bit about who's in the room makes that first team meeting significantly more productive. And the act of responding to someone else's post, rather than just reading it, practices the kind of active engagement that good teamwork depends on.
