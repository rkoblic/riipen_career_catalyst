# Video Script: The shift

Here's something worth knowing. Two years ago, most professionals outside of tech had never used an AI tool at work. Today, the majority have. That's not a gradual trend. That's one of the fastest shifts in how professional work gets done that we've ever seen.

And it's not just the obvious roles. Yes, software engineers use AI to write code. But so do grant writers drafting funding proposals, urban planners modeling neighborhood impact, journalists fact-checking across thousands of public records, and HR teams rethinking how they write job descriptions. AI didn't just show up in tech. It showed up everywhere, quickly.

Here's why that matters for you. Employers are already adjusting their expectations. The ability to work effectively with AI tools is becoming a baseline professional skill, the way spreadsheet literacy or business writing were for previous generations. Not "nice to have." Expected.

That doesn't mean you need to become a technical expert. It means you need to understand what these tools can and can't do, know when to use them and when to set them aside, and be able to explain and stand behind any work they helped you produce.

That's exactly what the AI modules in this course are designed to build. This isn't a sidebar topic. It's one of the most directly career-relevant skills you'll practice here.
