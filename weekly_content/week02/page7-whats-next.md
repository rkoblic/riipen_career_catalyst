# What's Next

The next major milestone in the course is the employer kickoff meeting, your team's first direct conversation with the professional contact you reached out to this week. Everything you've done so far (forming your team, researching the organization, practicing communication skills) leads directly to that meeting.

---

## Why Review the Rubric Now

Before you get to the kickoff, it's worth spending time with the Context Analysis & Project Plan assignment and rubric. This is the first graded deliverable in the course, and it's coming up soon after the kickoff.

Reviewing the rubric now is about knowing what to pay attention to. When you understand what the deliverable is asking for, you'll research with more direction, ask sharper questions at the kickoff, and notice things in the employer's answers that you'd otherwise miss.

Think of it as reading the recipe before you go grocery shopping. You don't cook yet, but you know what ingredients to look for.

## What to Look For in the Rubric

As you review, pay attention to:

- **What dimensions are being assessed.** The rubric evaluates specific types of thinking (research quality, analytical reasoning, strategic implications, actionability). Knowing these shapes what you prioritize.
- **What counts as evidence.** Each dimension has expectations for the kind of evidence and reasoning that earns strong marks. Notice what the rubric values and what it doesn't.
- **How this connects to your research.** The organizational research you're starting now feeds directly into this deliverable. The rubric tells you what kind of research will actually be useful vs. what will just be background noise.
