# What's next

You've just had your first real feedback conversation with the employer. You know what resonated, what shifted, and where the project is heading. That clarity is the setup for everything that comes next.

---

## The production phase

The Discovery phase is behind you. The next stretch of the course is production: building the deliverables you've been scoping and planning. Your approach has been tested against the employer's perspective, and you have a confirmed direction (or a deliberate revision) to work from. That's exactly the foundation you need before committing significant time to drafting, designing, and assembling.

Production is a different kind of work than what you've done so far. Discovery was about understanding the problem. Production is about solving it. The pace picks up, the work becomes more concrete, and the quality of your planning directly determines how smoothly things go.

---

## How to prepare

Use the remaining time this week to build the structural scaffolding for your deliverables. That means outlining your report sections, sketching a deck structure if your project calls for one, and dividing the work so every team member knows what they're responsible for producing.

This isn't busywork. Professional teams do this at the start of every production phase because it prevents the kind of last-minute scramble where three people write overlapping sections and nobody covers the gap in the middle. A clear structure now means fewer surprises later.
