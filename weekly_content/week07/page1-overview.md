# From draft to deliverable

Two things are happening this week at once: you're building out the professional skills your draft deliverables will need, and you're preparing for Check-In #1, the first progress meeting with your employer in the Development phase.

Check-In #1 is not a formal presentation. There's no slide deck, no rehearsed narrative, no performance of progress. It's a questions-based working session — your team brings specific questions and decision points, and the employer helps you move forward. The preparation that matters isn't polish; it's clarity about where you're stuck and what you need. The production skill modules this week are directly applicable to the deliverable you're building right now, and you'll draw on them every week through the end of the project.

## What's due this week

The **Work-in-Progress Summary** is a completion-based team checkpoint, submitted before Check-In #1. It's a preparation tool for your team, not a document the employer reads. The Check-In #1 page covers what goes in it and what the completion criteria require.

---

## Suggested order of completion

1. Complete the three production skill modules — From Plan to Product, Reviewing Your Teammates' Work, and Working with Data and Visuals. These build skills you'll apply immediately to your project work, and completing them before your team prep session means you can use the frameworks in practice.
2. Read the Receiving Feedback Well protocol and save the Feedback Protocol Card. Brief but useful context before the employer meeting.
3. Read the Work-in-Progress Summary description and completion criteria, then draft the summary as a team. This step is both the checkpoint deliverable and your meeting preparation.
4. Work through the Check-In #1 preparation materials — what this type of meeting is, how to develop useful questions, and how to anticipate what the employer might ask.
5. Hold your team prep session to finalize questions, assign leads for each topic, and plan for note-taking.
6. Check-In #1 meeting with your employer.
7. Post-meeting debrief immediately after — capture what was heard while it's fresh.
8. Continue project production, incorporating any direction from Check-In #1 where possible.
