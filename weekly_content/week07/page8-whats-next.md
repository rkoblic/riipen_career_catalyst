# What's next

You've just completed the first employer accountability point of the Development phase. The direction and feedback from Check-In #1 become your production priorities from here, and the peer review skills you practiced this week carry directly into what's coming next.

---

## Peer Evaluation #1

The first formal peer evaluation asks you to assess a teammate's contribution to the project — their draft work, their participation, and their collaboration. It's the same kind of structured, evidence-based feedback you practiced this week, applied to a teammate rather than a document section.

The quality of your evaluation depends on how closely you've been paying attention. Reviewers who have been engaged with their teammates' work — reading drafts, giving specific feedback, participating in the prep session and debrief — will have concrete observations to draw on. General impressions won't be enough.

---

## How to prepare

Review the Peer Evaluation #1 rubric before next week so you know what dimensions you'll be assessing. Keep notes as you work — specific observations about what a teammate produced, what you suggested, and how they responded are the raw material the evaluation requires. Your self-evaluation is part of the process too, so note your own contributions with the same specificity.
