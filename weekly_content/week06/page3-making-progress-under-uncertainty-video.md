# Video Script: Making progress under uncertainty

Here's something that takes most people a while to figure out in professional settings: the people who produce the best work aren't the ones who start with complete clarity. They're the ones who've learned to move without it.

Waiting for certainty before starting is an intuitive strategy. It feels responsible. But in practice, it means the work doesn't start — because complete certainty rarely arrives before a deadline does.

Experienced professionals don't wait. They develop a calibration: a sense of how much is enough to proceed, what kind of gap can be built around versus what kind of gap is actually blocking, and how to stay productive while something unresolved gets resolved.

That calibration is a skill. And like most skills, it's developed through practice — through noticing when you stop, asking yourself honestly whether you need to, and over time building a more accurate sense of your own thresholds.

The page that follows this video gives you a concrete framework for making those judgments. But before you read it, it's worth holding onto the underlying idea: working under incomplete information is not an obstacle to doing good professional work. It is what doing good professional work looks like most of the time.
