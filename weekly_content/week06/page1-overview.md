# From planning to production

Your approach is confirmed. Sections are assigned. Now comes the work of actually building something. This week marks the shift from coordinating and deciding to producing, and the goal is to end it with a named section drafted to a point where you can talk through it in a room.

## What's due this week

**Reflection #2** is the only graded deliverable this week (individual submission, approximately 5% of your final grade as part of the Reflection Portfolio). There's no employer touchpoint this week, which means your attention goes entirely to the modules and your production work.

Complete the reflection at the end of the week, after you've had time to work through the Discovery phase material.

---

## Suggested order of completion

Work through the modules before you start drafting or researching. The order matters. Each one is timed for a specific moment in the production process.

1. **Starting production as a team** — Before anyone opens the shared document. Covers the setup decisions your team needs to make together first: document structure, section ownership, formatting standards, and terminology. Skipping this creates problems that are hard to fix later.

2. **Formatting professional documents** — Immediately after setting up the document, before any content is added. Apply these standards to the template first, then write.

3. **Making progress under uncertainty** — Before you start your independent research or sit down to draft. Addresses the most common thing that stalls production: not knowing how to move forward when you don't have everything you need.

4. **From research to draft** — After your research is underway but before you try to write. Covers the step between notes and prose that most people skip, which is where most production stalls.

5. **Navigating disagreement** — Before your team's drafting check-in. Production surfaces disagreements that planning kept abstract.

After the modules: complete your independent research, then draft your assigned section. Reflection #2 comes last, after your full production experience.
