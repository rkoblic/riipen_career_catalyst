Critical Thinking Problem Framing — 2 weeks
Detailed Objectives by Week
▶ Week 1 Getting Started 5 objectives
#ObjectiveCompetencies
1Describe what distinguishes experiential learning in an AI-enabled professional context from traditional coursework.Self-Awareness
2Identify the skills and work habits that contribute to effective team performance in a professional project context.Shared Accountability
3Identify project opportunities that align with their interests and growth goals and submit preferences with written rationale.Prioritization Growth Orientation
4Describe professional email conventions appropriate to a workplace context.Professional Correspondence
5Identify the three stages of the DEAL reflective practice model and explain what each stage requires.Reflection
▶ Week 2 Building Your Team 5 objectives
#ObjectiveCompetencies
1Negotiate and document team operating agreements that establish shared expectations for communication, contribution, reliability, and accountability.Shared Accountability Collaborative Contribution Reliability
2Compose a professional outreach email to an employer contact that introduces the team and requests a kickoff meeting.Professional Correspondence
3Conduct initial organizational research on an employer to develop working knowledge of its context, sector, and project scope.Research & Analysis
4Identify the criteria and evidence required by a grading rubric in order to set direction for the Discovery phase.Prioritization
5Evaluate AI-generated output for accuracy, unsupported claims, and appropriate use in a professional project context.Evidence-Based Reasoning
▶ Week 3 Understanding Your Client 5 objectives
#ObjectiveCompetencies
1Conduct a professional employer kickoff meeting that demonstrates preparation, role clarity, and active listening.Active Listening Dependability
2Produce a kickoff summary that accurately documents confirmed scope, deliverables, success criteria, and open questions from the employer meeting.Written Clarity
3Distinguish between confirmed information and open questions in order to identify what additional research is needed after the kickoff.Evidence-Based Reasoning
4Conduct targeted post-kickoff research oriented to the specific project scope confirmed in the meeting.Research & Analysis
5Compose a DEAL reflection that examines a specific experience, identifying what happened, what it revealed, and what can be carried forward as transferable learning.Reflection
▶ Week 4 Context Analysis & Project Plan 5 objectives
#ObjectiveCompetencies
1Synthesize independent research findings into a coherent analytical point of view, distinguishing what was found from what it means for the project.Research & Analysis
2Construct evidence-based claims by identifying what counts as evidence, supporting assertions with sources, and acknowledging gaps and uncertainty.Evidence-Based Reasoning
3Translate research insights into strategic implications by naming assumptions, identifying constraints, and connecting analysis to a proposed project approach.Problem Framing
4Develop a project plan that reflects deliberate scoping decisions, realistic milestones, named risks, and explicit prioritization of what the team will and will not do.Prioritization
5Produce a professionally coherent team document that reads as a unified whole despite being drafted collaboratively across multiple contributors.Written Clarity
▶ Week 5 Professional Communication 4 objectives
#ObjectiveCompetencies
1Present a proposed approach to a professional stakeholder in a way that invites feedback rather than defends a position.Oral Presentation
2Conduct a structured employer meeting by arriving with prepared talking points, listening actively to feedback, and asking clarifying questions when input is vague or unexpected.Active Listening
3Translate verbal employer feedback into a written record that captures specific input, concrete plan adjustments, and confirmed next steps.Written Clarity
4Receive stakeholder feedback that requires a change in direction without defensiveness and incorporate it into a revised project approach.Feedback Reception
▶ Week 6 Working Through Ambiguity 4 objectives
#ObjectiveCompetencies
1Apply a practical decision framework for making progress when information is incomplete, recognizing when enough exists to proceed versus when additional research is needed.Prioritization
2Navigate a disagreement with a teammate or an unexpected employer direction using a professional approach that maintains relationships while surfacing the substantive issue.Constructive Feedback Feedback Reception
3Organize research notes into a structured outline and use that outline as the basis for drafting, applying a deliberate process that separates synthesis from production.Research & Analysis Written Clarity
4Compose a DEAL reflection that examines a specific experience from the Discovery phase, demonstrating increased depth in the Examine stage.Reflection
▶ Week 7 Building Professional Work Products 5 objectives
#ObjectiveCompetencies
1Apply a structured protocol for receiving feedback on their own work, including listening to understand, clarifying before reacting, and deferring evaluation until after the conversation.Feedback Reception
2Distinguish between the conventions of academic writing and professional client-facing deliverables and apply those conventions to their own draft work products.Written Clarity Quality & Attention to Detail
3Read a teammate's draft section and provide specific, actionable written feedback focused on clarity, argument structure, evidence quality, and tone.Constructive Feedback
4Make deliberate decisions about when to use tables, charts, or narrative to present data in a way that supports an argument rather than simply reports information.Written Clarity Evidence-Based Reasoning
5Produce a concise written summary of project progress that honestly assesses current status, identifies specific challenges, and articulates targeted questions for the employer.Written Clarity
▶ Week 8 From Feedback to Forward Motion 4 objectives
#ObjectiveCompetencies
1Sort employer feedback into categories of act on, note, or set aside, and translate prioritized feedback into a concrete revision plan with assigned responsibilities.Feedback Reception Prioritization
2Revise previously submitted work in response to employer feedback, distinguishing between feedback that requires structural changes and feedback addressable through targeted edits.Feedback Reception Written Clarity
3Evaluate each teammate's contribution using behaviorally anchored rating scales, providing specific behavioral evidence for each rating and qualitative feedback.Constructive Feedback Shared Accountability
4Distinguish between AI uses that support production and AI uses that substitute for thinking, and articulate how to maintain ownership of AI-assisted work.Evidence-Based Reasoning
▶ Week 9 Strengthening Your Work 5 objectives
#ObjectiveCompetencies
1Identify where a multi-author document reads as disconnected sections and apply specific techniques to integrate work produced by different teammates into a coherent deliverable.Written Clarity
2Evaluate a draft deliverable against an actionability standard, determining whether recommendations are specific enough for the employer to act on, realistic given constraints, and prioritized.Prioritization Written Clarity
3Make deliberate scope decisions when mid-project pressures to expand arise, distinguishing between work that belongs in the current deliverable, work that should be deferred, and work that should be declined.Prioritization Initiative
4Synthesize feedback from multiple internal and external sources to identify patterns across perspectives and negotiate specific behavioral changes with the team.Constructive Feedback Shared Accountability
5Compose a DEAL reflection with greater depth and specificity than previous attempts, focusing on a moment involving feedback or collaboration.Reflection
▶ Week 10 Connecting Theory and Practice 4 objectives
#ObjectiveCompetencies
1Distinguish between project-specific knowledge and transferable professional competencies developed through the project experience.Self-Awareness
2Extract transferable principles from specific project experiences, articulating learning in terms that apply beyond this particular project and employer.Growth Orientation Career Articulation Evidence-Based Reasoning
3Maintain a change log that documents feedback received, how it was addressed, and rationale for feedback not incorporated.Feedback Reception Quality & Attention to Detail
4Use AI as a quality reviewer to evaluate their own draft work for consistency, logical gaps, tone mismatches, and reader confusion.Prioritization
▶ Week 11 Quality and Craft 4 objectives
#ObjectiveCompetencies
1Distinguish between work that is complete and work that is polished, applying professional quality standards to evaluate their own deliverable package.Quality & Attention to Detail
2Conduct a systematic self-review of a multi-section deliverable, identifying errors, inconsistencies, and alignment gaps that undermine credibility with a professional audience.Quality & Attention to Detail Prioritization
3Prepare a deliverable package for employer review and develop targeted questions appropriate to the near-final stage of a project.Active Listening Feedback Reception
4Discuss near-final deliverables with the employer, demonstrating how prior feedback was incorporated and surfacing specific remaining questions about quality and completeness.Professional Correspondence Feedback Reception
▶ Week 12 From Feedback to Final 4 objectives
#ObjectiveCompetencies
1Distinguish between feedback that requires revision and feedback that can be acknowledged without action, making deliberate decisions about what to change, what to defer, and when the deliverable is done.Feedback Reception Prioritization
2Apply presentation fundamentals including audience awareness, narrative structure, visual clarity, and speaker coordination to plan a team presentation of completed project work.Oral Presentation
3Produce a DEAL reflection that connects specific project experiences to broader professional understanding, examining the gap between the original plan and the work actually produced.Reflection Evidence-Based Reasoning
4Finalize a deliverable package that incorporates near-final review feedback selectively and intentionally, with changes documented and the overall package approaching submission-ready quality.Quality & Attention to Detail Feedback Reception
▶ Week 13 Finalizing Deliverables 2 objectives
#ObjectiveCompetencies
1Execute a systematic pre-submission review of a multi-component deliverable package, verifying formatting consistency, rubric alignment, and completeness before final submission.Quality & Attention to Detail
2Distinguish between targeted final adjustments that improve a deliverable and last-minute overhauls that introduce new problems, applying judgment about when work is ready to submit.Quality & Attention to Detail Prioritization
▶ Week 14 Preparing to Present and Looking Ahead 4 objectives
#ObjectiveCompetencies
1Plan and build a team presentation that translates deliverable content into a narrative structure appropriate for an employer audience, with clear role assignments and coordinated transitions.Oral Presentation
2Anticipate and prepare for audience questions, including questions the team cannot fully answer, by developing strategies for responding under pressure without fabricating or deflecting.Active Listening Initiative
3Extract transferable professional principles from the project experience, distinguishing between project-specific knowledge and insight that applies to future professional contexts.Reflection Career Articulation
4Evaluate each teammate's contributions across the full project with sufficient behavioral evidence to support honest, differentiated ratings.Constructive Feedback Shared Accountability
▶ Week 15 Presenting Your Work 5 objectives
#ObjectiveCompetencies
1Deliver a formal, team-coordinated presentation of completed project work to the employer, communicating findings and recommendations through a coherent narrative.Oral Presentation
2Respond to audience questions from genuine understanding during a formal presentation setting.Active Listening Quality & Attention to Detail
3Produce a synthesis reflection that identifies the most important professional insight from the course and articulates it with precision, depth, and transferability.Reflection Growth Orientation
4Complete the summative peer evaluation, assessing each teammate's contributions across the full project with specific behavioral evidence and honest differentiation.Constructive Feedback Shared Accountability
5Articulate project contributions and professional growth in terms appropriate for resumes, interviews, and professional networking contexts.Career Articulation
