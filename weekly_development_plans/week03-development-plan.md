# Week 3 — Understanding Your Client
## Content Development Plan

---

## Content Architecture

Week 3 is organized around a single anchor event — the employer kickoff meeting — making this a **Pattern A (meeting-centered)** week. The kickoff is the first direct employer interaction in the course, so all module content either prepares students for it or helps them process it afterward. The week reduces to **four content pieces**: a Week Overview, a Kickoff Meeting & Summary anchor prompt structured as before/during/after, a What's Next transitional brief previewing Week 4, and a Reflection #1 prompt (Week 3 is the first reflection week). Nearly every activity absorbs into the anchor prompt; the only exception is async admin time.

---

## Content Production Plan

### 1. Week 3 Overview
**Type:** Orientation text
**Absorbs:** 3.01
**Format:** AI-generated video (~60–90 sec) + short orientation text

Frames the week around the employer kickoff meeting as the anchor event. Names both deliverables (Kickoff Summary, Reflection #1) and their stakes — the Summary is completion-based, the Reflection is the first graded individual submission (~5%). Gives a suggested order of completion that makes the before/during/after rhythm visible.

- Why this week matters: your first real professional interaction with the employer (AI-generated video; ~60–90 sec)
- What's due, when, and in what order (AI-generated text; ~150 words)

---

### 2. Kickoff Meeting & Summary Anchor Prompt
**Type:** Assignment prompt with embedded guidance
**Absorbs:** 3.02, 3.03, 3.04, 3.05, 3.06, 3.07, 3.08, 3.09, 3.10, 3.11, 3.12, 3.13, 3.14, 3.15

The anchor prompt carries the instructional weight for the week. Organized in three phases around the kickoff meeting, plus a deliverable section and a post-kickoff research section.

#### Before the Meeting

- Professional video call setup and etiquette — camera, lighting, background, muting, handling crosstalk (curated link + framing; ~1 sentence)
- Setting up AI note capture: how to use AI transcription/summarization tools during or immediately after the meeting; how to prompt AI to organize raw notes (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- Pre-meeting prep: review your Week 2 research, consolidate and prioritize questions as a team, assign roles — who asks, who takes notes, who manages time (checklist)
- Team huddle guidance: what to align on in your pre-meeting sync — confirm questions, confirm roles, do a quick rehearsal (AI-generated text; ~100 words)
- Email to employer: confirming logistics, platform/tech setup, any pre-meeting clarifications (template)

#### During the Meeting

- What to expect: the employer presents context, scope, success criteria, timeline, and key contacts; your job is to listen and ask prepared questions (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- Interactive kickoff scenario: simulated employer kickoff with interpretation questions — what did the employer actually confirm vs. what are you assuming? (interactive practice)
- What to listen for and how to take notes while staying engaged on camera (checklist)

#### After the Meeting

- Immediate debrief: what surprised you, what you need to find out, what the employer seemed to care most about — capture while impressions are fresh (AI-generated text; ~100 words)
- Processing what you heard: turning raw notes into shared team understanding; reconciling different interpretations of what was said (AI-generated text; ~200 words)
- How teams synthesize meeting notes in professional practice (curated link + framing; source article or video, ~5 min reading/viewing)
- Following up professionally: the post-meeting thank-you and confirmation email (template)
- Identifying gaps and open questions: distinguishing confirmed information from assumptions; what additional research is needed (AI-generated text; ~150 words)

#### Deliverable: Kickoff Summary

- What the Kickoff Summary is for: a factual record, not analysis or interpretation (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- Kickoff Summary format: confirmed scope, deliverables, success criteria, timeline, key contacts, open questions (template)
- Completion criteria and submission requirements (AI-generated text; ~100 words)
- AI disclosure expectations: AI-assisted organization is permitted and encouraged; AI cannot generate the substance of what was discussed (AI-generated text; ~75 words)

#### Post-Kickoff Research

- Targeted landscape research primer: now that scope is confirmed, where to look and what you're trying to understand — Part 2 of the research progression (Week 2 = reconnaissance, Week 3 = targeted landscape, Week 4 = analytical synthesis) (AI-generated text; ~200 words)
- How to organize findings before you can synthesize them — feeding into Week 4's Context Analysis (AI-generated text; ~100 words)
- Researching an industry or competitive landscape (curated link + framing; source article or video, ~5 min reading/viewing)

### 3. What's Next
**Type:** Transitional prompt
**Absorbs:** 3.16
**Format:** AI-generated text

Brief bridge to Week 4. Previews the Context Analysis & Project Plan — what it is, what goes in it, and how the Kickoff Summary and post-kickoff research feed directly into it. Forward preparation only; not full instruction. Links to the assignment and rubric so students can begin orienting.

- What's coming in Week 4 and how this week's work feeds it (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- Linked resource: Context Analysis & Project Plan assignment description and rubric (students review in advance; ~10–15 min)

---

### 4. Reflection #1 Prompt
**Type:** Reflection prompt
**Absorbs:** 3.18
**Format:** Assignment prompt with embedded reference material

First graded DEAL reflection in the course (~5% of final grade). Students draw from their Foundation phase experience (Weeks 1–3) — the kickoff meeting is the most likely anchor experience but students are not limited to it.

- Why reflection matters in professional practice (AI-generated text; ~100 words)
- DEAL framework reference sheet: Describe → Examine → Articulate Learning (AI-generated text; ~200 words)
- Pro tip: how to use AI as a thinking partner for reflection without letting it supply the experience or the learning (AI-generated text; ~100 words)
- Rubric dimensions: Accurate Description, Critical Examination, Honest Self-Assessment, Articulation of Learning — expectations calibrated to a first attempt (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- Submission requirements and AI disclosure (AI-generated text; ~75 words)
