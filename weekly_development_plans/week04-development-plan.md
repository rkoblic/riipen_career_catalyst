# Week 4 — Context Analysis & Project Plan
## Content Development Plan

---

## Content Architecture

Week 4 is a **Pattern B (production-centered)** week. The entire week revolves around producing the Context Analysis & Project Plan — the first graded deliverable of the course (10% of final grade). All six module activities (4.02–4.07) serve the anchor deliverable directly, teaching the analytical skills required to move from information-gathering to evidence-based argument. The anchor prompt is the heaviest in the course so far, organized around the deliverable's four parts and the production workflow. No employer meeting, no reflection. The flow is: Overview → Context Analysis & Project Plan Anchor Prompt → What's Next (bridging to the Week 5 Direction Check).

---

## Content Production Plan

### 1. Week 4 Overview
**Type:** Orientation text
**Absorbs:** 4.01

Frames the week around the first graded deliverable. Names the cognitive shift — from gathering information (Weeks 2–3) to arguing from it. Gives suggested order of completion and makes the production workflow visible: research → synthesis → drafting → review → integration → submission.

- Why this deliverable matters: your first graded submission and the analytical foundation for everything that follows (AI-generated video; ~60–90 sec)
- What's due, when, and the production workflow for the week (AI-generated text; ~200 words)

---

### 2. Context Analysis & Project Plan Anchor Prompt
**Type:** Assignment prompt with embedded guidance
**Absorbs:** 4.02, 4.03, 4.04, 4.05, 4.06, 4.07

The anchor prompt carries all the instructional weight this week. Organized by the deliverable's four parts, with production workflow guidance and AI use embedded throughout. Each section maps to a rubric dimension so students can see the connection between what they're learning and how they'll be assessed.

#### Part 1: Organizational & Project Context

- What this section is for: establishing that you understand the organization and project well enough to analyze it — not restating the brief, but demonstrating working knowledge (AI-generated text; ~200 words)
- What to include: organization overview, sector context, project scope as confirmed at the kickoff, key stakeholders (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- The difference between restating what you were told and demonstrating understanding (AI-generated text; ~100 words)

#### Part 2: Research Findings (absorbs 4.02, 4.03)

- Why this section matters: moving from "what we found" to "what it means" — the shift from summary to synthesis (AI-generated video; ~60–90 sec)
- Synthesizing research into insights: identifying patterns across findings, drawing conclusions, avoiding the summary trap (AI-generated text; ~300 words)
- What counts as evidence: supporting claims vs. asserting them, acknowledging gaps and uncertainty (AI-generated text; ~250 words)
- How professionals structure research findings (curated link + framing; source article or video, ~5 min reading/viewing)
- Common pitfalls: what weak research sections look like vs. strong ones (AI-generated text; ~200 words)

#### Part 3: Strategic Implications (absorbs 4.04)

- The "so what" move: connecting research findings to a strategic point of view on the project — why this is the skill that separates summary from strategy (AI-generated video; ~60–90 sec)
- Naming assumptions, identifying constraints, and explaining why your proposed approach follows from what you found (AI-generated text; ~200 words)
- What strategic thinking looks like in practice (curated link + framing; source article or video, ~5 min reading/viewing)

#### Part 4: Project Plan (absorbs 4.05, 4.06)

- What a project plan is and isn't: deliberate scoping decisions, not a wishlist (AI-generated text; ~200 words)
- Prioritization: making tradeoffs explicit — what you will do, what you won't, and why (AI-generated text; ~200 words)
- Milestones and timeline: realistic sequencing that reflects your actual capacity (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- Risk identification and mitigation: what goes wrong in projects, likelihood vs. impact thinking, realistic mitigation strategies (AI-generated text; ~250 words)
- Risk identification frameworks (curated link + framing; source article or video, ~5 min reading/viewing)

#### Using AI for Research and Synthesis (absorbs 4.07)

- How to use AI for competitive landscape and industry research: prompting strategies, evaluating output, verifying claims (AI-generated text; ~200 words)
- Example prompts: competitive landscape overview and cross-team synthesis (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- What AI cannot do: the defend-your-work standard and the Direction Check accountability mechanism (AI-generated text; ~100 words)

#### Production Workflow

- How to get from research to finished document as a team: individual research → team synthesis meeting → individual drafting → async cross-review → integration pass → final review (AI-generated text; ~250 words)
- The integration pass: making it read as one document — unified voice, transitions, no contradictions (AI-generated text; ~200 words)
- Self-directed skill acquisition: learning unfamiliar methods like competitive analysis, stakeholder mapping, and risk matrices (AI-generated text; ~100 words)
- What is a competitive analysis? (curated link + framing; source article or video, ~5–8 min reading/viewing)
- What is stakeholder mapping? (curated link + framing; source article or video, ~5–8 min reading/viewing)

#### Submission

- Deliverable format: four parts, approximate page lengths (AI-generated text; ~100 words)
- Rubric overview: Communication Quality (20%), Analytical Rigor (25%), Actionability (20%), Professionalism (15%), Project-Specific Dimension (20%) (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- AI disclosure requirements (AI-generated text; ~75 words)
- Submission instructions (AI-generated text; ~50 words)

### 3. What's Next
**Type:** Transitional prompt

Bridges to Week 5. The Direction Check is the next employer touchpoint — the employer will have read the Context Analysis & Project Plan and will be asking questions. Frames this as an accountability moment, not an evaluation: pivots based on employer feedback are expected and don't reflect poorly on the submission.

- What's coming in Week 5: the Direction Check — your employer has read your work and wants to talk about it (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- How to prepare: be ready to explain and defend every claim in your document (AI-generated text; ~100 words)
