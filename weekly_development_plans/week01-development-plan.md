# Week 1 — Getting Started
## Content Development Plan

---

## Content Architecture

Week 1 is a **Pattern E (orientation/formation)** week. There is no employer touchpoint or major graded deliverable — the Student Intake Questionnaire is completion-based and the preference submission is a platform task. No single anchor dominates, and the week is heavy on foundational modules introducing skills and frameworks students will need in later weeks. As a result, more content stays as standalone modules than in a typical week. The anchor prompt is lighter, organizing around project browsing and the intake/preference submission. The flow is: Overview → Course Foundation modules → Project Browsing & Intake Anchor → Professional Skill Primers → What's Next (bridging to Week 2 team formation).

---

## Content Production Plan

### 1. Week 1 Overview
**Type:** Orientation text
**Absorbs:** 1.01, 1.09

Welcomes students to the course. Frames the week as orientation and exploration — no team, no project yet. Names the deliverable (Student Intake Questionnaire + preference submission) and gives a suggested order of completion. Includes the LMS introduction post prompt as a community-building activity (post a self-intro, respond to 2–3 peers).

- Why this course is different: what to expect from experiential learning (AI-generated video; ~60–90 sec)
- What's happening this week, what's due, and suggested completion order (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- LMS introduction post prompt: introduce yourself and respond to peers (AI-generated text; ~75 words)

---

### 2. What Is Experiential Learning?
**Type:** Standalone module
**Absorbs:** 1.02

Frames the experiential learning cycle and sets expectations for how this course differs from traditional coursework. Students need to understand the "why" behind the course structure before they encounter team-based project work.

- Why experiential learning matters in an AI-enabled workplace (AI-generated text; ~200 words)
- How learning works in this course vs. traditional coursework — the cycle of experience → reflection → application (AI-generated video; ~60–90 sec)
- Deeper dive: what experiential learning looks like in practice (curated link + framing; source article or video, ~5 min reading/viewing)
- What this means for you as a student: different expectations, different habits (AI-generated text; ~150 words)

---

### 3. Syllabus & Assessment Review
**Type:** Standalone module
**Absorbs:** 1.03

Walks students through the syllabus, grading structure, and assessment approach. Foundational for academic integrity and student planning. This is a 40-minute activity — the longest orientation module — because students spend most of the time reading the actual syllabus document; our content frames and guides that reading.

- Linked resource: course syllabus document (students read the full syllabus; ~15–20 min)
- Course arc overview: the three phases (Foundation → Production → Delivery) and what happens in each (AI-generated text; ~250 words)
- Assessment structure: what's graded, what's completion-based, how the reflection portfolio works (AI-generated text; ~300 words)
- Key policies: attendance, late submissions, academic integrity (AI-generated text; ~200 words)
- Deliverable timeline: when things are due across all 15 weeks (AI-generated text or table; ~200 words)

---

### 4. AI in Today's Workplace & This Course
**Type:** Standalone module
**Absorbs:** 1.04

Introduces the AI-enabled workplace context and the course's AI policy. This is the policy foundation — students are expected to have read it before Week 2 when graded work begins.

- AI in professional work: what's changed and why it matters for your career (AI-generated video; ~60–90 sec)
- How professionals are using AI right now (curated link + framing; source article or video, ~5–8 min reading/viewing)
- This course's AI policy: what's permitted, what requires disclosure, and the defend-your-work standard (AI-generated text; ~300 words)
- The one hard rule: where AI is off-limits and why (AI-generated text; ~100 words)
- Quick-reference AI policy summary (checklist)

---

### 5. Project Browsing & Student Intake Anchor Prompt
**Type:** Assignment prompt with embedded guidance
**Absorbs:** 1.05, 1.07, 1.08

The anchor prompt for the week, though lighter than a typical anchor. Orients students to the project-based structure, guides them through reviewing briefs, and frames the intake questionnaire and preference submission.

#### Understanding Your Project & Team
- How projects work in this course: team structure, employer relationship, deliverable arc (AI-generated text; ~200 words)
- What to expect from your team experience (AI-generated text; ~150 words)

#### Reviewing Project Briefs
- How to skim first, then deep-read: a strategy for evaluating multiple briefs without anchoring on the first one (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- What to look for in a brief: scope, skills required, industry context, what excites you (checklist)
- Using AI to compare briefs: generating summary questions or a comparison framework (AI-generated text; ~100 words)

#### Student Intake Questionnaire & Preference Submission
- What the intake questionnaire is for: project matching, reflection baselines, and goal-tracking throughout the course (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- Why this must be completed without AI: honest self-assessment feeds the personalization chain for all five reflections (AI-generated text; ~100 words)
- Writing your preference rationale: how to justify your project selections with reasoning, not just preference (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- Submission requirements (AI-generated text; ~75 words)

---

### 6. Introduction to Reflective Practice (DEAL)
**Type:** Standalone module
**Absorbs:** 1.10

Introduces the DEAL reflective practice model that students will use five times across the course (Weeks 3, 6, 9, 12, 15). Positioned as a professional skill, not just an academic exercise. Students encounter the framework here; they first apply it in Week 3.

- Why reflective practice matters in professional work (AI-generated video; ~60–90 sec)
- The DEAL model: Describe → Examine → Articulate Learning — what each stage requires (AI-generated text; ~250 words)
- Worked example: a sample DEAL reflection showing what good looks like (AI-generated text; ~300 words)

---

### 7. Setting Yourself Up for Success
**Type:** Standalone module
**Absorbs:** 1.11

Time management and planning basics for project-based work. Generic professional skill, but framed in the context of sustained project work rather than exam cramming.

- Why project-based work requires different time habits than traditional coursework (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- Practical time management strategies for distributed, team-based work (curated link + framing; source external article/video, ~5–8 min reading/viewing)
- Planning your week: how to break project work into manageable blocks (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- Weekly planning template: a simple worksheet for mapping tasks to time blocks (template)

---

### 8. Professional Email Basics
**Type:** Standalone module
**Absorbs:** 1.13

Practical skill introduced before students need it — applies immediately in Week 2 when teams send outreach to their employer contact. Generic professional skill, so curated external resources carry the load.

- Why email still matters in professional communication (AI-generated text; ~100 words)
- Email structure, tone, and context-sensitivity (curated link + framing; source external article/video)
- Common mistakes and how to avoid them in a workplace context (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- Example: a well-structured professional email annotated with what makes it work (scenario card)

---

### 9. What's Next
**Type:** Transitional prompt
**Absorbs:** 1.14, 1.15

Bridges to Week 2 team formation. Gives students pre-exposure to team norms and the Team Charter so they arrive primed for the charter conversation with shared vocabulary.

- What's coming in Week 2: you'll meet your team and build your working agreement (AI-generated text; ~100 words)
- Pre-reading: what team norms are and why they matter (curated link + framing; source article or video on team norms/working agreements, ~10–15 min reading/viewing)
- Reflection prompts: what kind of teammate do you want to be? What matters to you in how a team works together? (AI-generated text; ~150 words)
- Team Charter template preview: what it contains and what you'll be asked to do with it — linked to the actual template for advance review (AI-generated text; ~100 words)
- Linked resource: Team Charter template (students review the template in advance; ~10–15 min)
