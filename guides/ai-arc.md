# AI Skills Arc — Riipen Career Catalyst

Reference document mapping every AI touchpoint across the 15-week course. Used for content production planning, not student-facing.

**Content status:** Weeks 1–6 have produced content. Weeks 7–15 are design doc only.

---

## The arc at a glance

| Phase | Weeks | Focus | Key shift |
|-------|-------|-------|-----------|
| **Foundation & policy** | 1–3 | What AI is, what's permitted, how to prompt well, how to evaluate output | From "I've used ChatGPT" to "I know how to use AI as a professional tool" |
| **Research & organization** | 4–6 | AI for landscape research, synthesis of findings, notes-to-outline-to-draft | From "AI generates content for me" to "AI helps me organize my own thinking" |
| **Production & refinement** | 7–9 | AI as production partner: structural review, self-editing, gap-finding, actionability testing | From "AI helps me start" to "AI helps me improve what I've built" |
| **Quality review & integration** | 10–13 | AI as quality reviewer: reading own work fresh, consistency checking, identifying weak points | From "AI helps me build" to "AI helps me evaluate" |
| **Synthesis & translation** | 14–15 | Socratic partner for deep reflection, professional self-presentation, defend-your-work in live Q&A | From "AI helps my project" to "AI helps my career" |

## Recurring principles (apply every week)

- **Defend-your-work standard:** If you can explain and defend it, it's yours regardless of tools used. If you can't, it doesn't belong in your work. Introduced Week 1, reinforced at every graded deliverable.
- **Draft first, AI refines:** Students write first, then use AI to polish. Not the other way around. Introduced Week 2.
- **AI disclosure:** Required on every graded submission regardless of extent of AI use. "If AI was not used, a one-sentence statement is sufficient."
- **Verify AI-generated facts:** AI's training data has a cutoff. For anything time-sensitive or organization-specific, go to primary sources. Introduced Week 2, reinforced whenever research is involved.
- **Data privacy:** Don't paste confidential employer information into AI tools. Public info and your own work are fine. Employer-specific confidential data should be paraphrased or anonymized. Introduced Week 1.
- **Bias awareness:** AI reflects biases in training data. Output about people, communities, and demographics requires the same critical eye as any single source. Introduced Week 2, relevant whenever research involves people or communities.
- **AI amplifies skill, doesn't replace it:** AI makes skilled researchers/writers more efficient but doesn't make unskilled ones skilled. Introduced Week 4.

## Gaps and observations

### Addressed in content (as of April 2026)
- **How AI works:** Mental model added to Week 1 (pattern-matching, not reasoning). Grounds all subsequent guidance.
- **Data privacy:** Added to Week 1. Covers public info vs. own work vs. employer confidential data.
- **Bias:** Added to Week 2. Covers default assumptions, missing perspectives, stereotyping in AI output.
- **When not to use AI:** Added to Week 2. Covers skill-building, thinking shortcuts, process-vs-output distinction.
- **AI as amplifier:** Added to Week 4. Makes explicit that AI makes skilled researchers better but doesn't make unskilled researchers skilled.
- **Advanced prompting techniques:** Added to Week 4. Covers iterative prompting, chain of thought, few-shot examples, and custom GPTs/Gems/Projects.

### Still to watch during Weeks 7-15 production
- **Weeks 7-15 are design doc only.** The AI arc described in the design docs is well-structured but will need to be translated into the same content patterns established in Weeks 1-6 (AI at point of need, example prompts, accountability mechanisms).
- **The "production partner" shift (Week 8)** and "quality reviewer" capstone (Week 10) are important arc moments that will need dedicated attention during content production.
- **AI ethics beyond privacy and bias** — The course doesn't address intellectual property, AI and labor displacement, or broader societal implications. This is probably appropriate for a professional skills course (vs. an AI ethics course), but worth noting.
- **Tool-specific guidance** may need updating as AI tools evolve. The custom GPT/Gem/Projects guidance references specific product names that could change.
- **Week 3 content vs. design doc:** Design doc mentions AI for research more prominently than the produced content does. The content integrates AI guidance into the research page but doesn't have a dedicated AI section. This is probably fine — it follows the "AI at point of need" principle.
