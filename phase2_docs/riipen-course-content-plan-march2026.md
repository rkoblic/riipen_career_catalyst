## Overview

This document outlines the module content strategy for the 15-week Riipen Experience course. It serves as a content brief for module development — specifying what is taught each week, why, and how it connects to deliverables and competencies. The Course Structure document owns the structural picture (phases, deliverables, employer touchpoints); this document owns the module-level detail.

Content is designed to support competency development and project activities, with practical skills front-loaded and conceptual depth introduced when students have experience to connect it to.

### Design Principles

1. **Variable module time based on need** — Heavier content during orientation and skill-building weeks; lighter during production-intensive weeks
2. **Practical skills seeded early** — Students need professional email, research skills, and team collaboration before the modules that go deep on those topics
3. **Just-in-time preparation** — Content aligned to upcoming activities (kickoff prep before kickoff, feedback skills before progress review)
4. **Competency coverage verified** — All NACE competency subskills explicitly addressed in module content

### Time Budget

- **Total module content:** ~13 hours across 15 weeks
- **Weekly course total:** ~9 hours (3-credit course)
- **Balance:** As module time decreases in later weeks, project work time increases

---

## Content Functions

Module content serves five distinct functions:

| Function | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Orientation** | What is this course? How does experiential learning work? | Week 1 |
| **Skill-building** | How do I do [specific thing] well? | Research, communication, feedback, presentation |
| **Just-in-time prep** | What do I need to know for [upcoming activity]? | Kickoff prep, progress review prep |
| **Conceptual framework** | How do I make sense of what I'm experiencing? | Ambiguity, theory-practice connection, transfer |
| **Reflection scaffolding** | How do I reflect meaningfully using DEAL? | Weeks 3, 6, 9, 12, 15 |

## Week-by-Week Content Plan

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1–3)

#### Week 1: Getting Started

**Module Time:** 90 minutes (Heavy)

**Primary Function:** Orientation + Practical skill-building

| Section | Time | Content |
|---|---|---|
| What Is Experiential Learning? | 20 min | Brief orientation: how this course differs from traditional courses, the learning cycle, what's expected of you |
| Course Structure & Expectations | 15 min | 15-week arc, deliverables, employer touchpoints, how you'll be assessed |
| Creating an Effective Team Charter | 25 min | Why charters matter, what to include, how to have the conversation; includes reliability/dependability and contribution expectations |
| Time Management for Project Work | 20 min | Managing ongoing work (not cramming), prioritization basics, working with teammates across schedules |
| Professional Email Basics | 10 min | Quick intro: structure, tone, when to email vs. other channels; examples of good/bad emails |

**Competencies Supported:**
- Teamwork: Reliability, Collaborative contribution, Shared accountability (via charter content)
- Professionalism: Dependability (via time management)
- Critical Thinking: Prioritization (via time management)
- Communication: Professional correspondence (intro)

**Deliverable Connection:** Student Intake Questionnaire (Checkpoint)

---

#### Week 2: Preparing for Your Client

**Module Time:** 75 minutes (Medium-Heavy)

**Primary Function:** Skill-building (Research) + Just-in-time (Kickoff prep)

| Section | Time | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Researching an Organization | 25 min | What to look for, where to find it, how deep to go; industry context, company specifics, recent news |
| Preparing for a Client Meeting | 20 min | What makes a kickoff successful, setting agenda, role assignments for the meeting |
| Asking Good Questions | 15 min | Open vs. closed questions, questions that clarify vs. questions that impress, prioritizing your questions |
| Active Listening in Client Meetings | 15 min | How to listen (not just wait to talk), note-taking strategies, clarifying without interrupting |

**Competencies Supported:**
- Critical Thinking: Research and analysis
- Communication: Active listening, Professional correspondence
- Professionalism: Initiative (via preparation)

**Deliverable Connection:** Team Charter + Employer Outreach Email (Checkpoint)

---

#### Week 3: Understanding Your Client

**Module Time:** 45 minutes (Light)

**Primary Function:** Processing + Skill reinforcement

| Section | Time | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Processing What You Learned | 20 min | Turning meeting notes into shared understanding, identifying what you learned vs. what's still unclear |
| Following Up Professionally | 15 min | The post-meeting email, confirming understanding, asking clarifying questions after the fact |
| Identifying Gaps and Ambiguity | 10 min | What don't you know yet? What assumptions are you making? Setting up research priorities |

**Competencies Supported:**
- Communication: Written clarity, Active listening (reinforcement)
- Critical Thinking: Problem framing (early exposure)

**Deliverable Connection:** Kickoff Summary (Checkpoint) + **Reflection #1 (GRADED)**

**Note:** Light module time because students just had their kickoff meeting — they have substantial processing, research, and reflection work to do. The DEAL framework is introduced in the Week 1 orientation content; Reflection #1 is the first application of that framework. The DEAL reference sheet and a pro tip on using AI for reflection are embedded in the Reflection #1 assignment prompt.

## Notes for Implementation

1. **Module content is asynchronous** — All videos, readings, and light activities are self-paced
2. **Reflection scaffolding is distributed across the course** — DEAL is introduced in Week 1 orientation content and applied at Weeks 3, 6, 9, 12, and 15; deeper DEAL scaffolding modules land at Weeks 6 and 10
3. **Practical skills come before conceptual depth** — Email basics in Week 1, deeper communication strategy in Week 5
4. **Light weeks = heavy production** — When module time decreases, project work time increases
5. **Content prepares for upcoming activities** — Feedback content before Progress Review, presentation content before Final Presentation
6. **Some content delivered as embedded activity prompts** — Not all skills warrant a module section; standing prompts in weekly activities (e.g., weekly progress check-ins, Change Log maintenance) deliver recurring professional habits without module overhead
