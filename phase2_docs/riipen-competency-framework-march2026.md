# Riipen Experience: Competency Framework

*How we define, develop, and assess professional competencies in project-based experiential learning*

*Updated February 2026*

---

## Overview

The Riipen Experience develops career-ready professional competencies through structured, employer-connected project work. This framework defines what students develop, how we assess it, and why it matters.

We use five competencies from NACE (National Association of Colleges and Employers), the recognized standard for career readiness. By adopting an established framework, Riipen Experience outcomes speak a language institutions and employers already understand.

For each competency, we identify specific subskills that are observable and assessable in the context of project-based work. These aren't abstract aspirations---they're concrete behaviors students demonstrate through their project deliverables, reflections, team collaboration, and professional engagement.

---

## Professional Competencies

Every Riipen Experience develops and assesses five NACE Career Readiness Competencies. For each competency, we've identified the subskills most relevant to project-based client work---behaviors that are observable in deliverables, team interactions, professional engagement, and reflection.

---

### Communication

*Clearly and effectively exchange information, ideas, facts, and perspectives with persons inside and outside of an organization.*

| Subskill | Description |
|---|---|
| **Written clarity** | Produce clear, organized written work appropriate for the audience and purpose |
| **Oral presentation** | Present ideas effectively in meetings and formal presentations |
| **Professional correspondence** | Communicate promptly, professionally, and appropriately via email and other channels |
| **Active listening** | Listen to understand, ask clarifying questions, confirm understanding before responding |

---

### Critical Thinking

*Identify and respond to needs based upon an understanding of situational context and logical analysis of relevant information.*

| Subskill | Description |
|---|---|
| **Problem framing** | Identify and define problems clearly before jumping to solutions |
| **Research and analysis** | Gather and synthesize information from multiple sources to inform decisions |
| **Evidence-based reasoning** | Support conclusions with evidence; distinguish between supported claims and assumptions |
| **Prioritization** | Identify what matters most when not everything can be done; allocate effort accordingly |

---

### Teamwork

*Build and maintain collaborative relationships to work effectively toward common goals, while appreciating diverse viewpoints and shared responsibilities.*

| Subskill | Description |
|---|---|
| **Reliability** | Deliver on commitments to teammates; do what you said you would do |
| **Collaborative contribution** | Contribute fair share of work; step up when team needs support |
| **Constructive feedback** | Give specific, actionable feedback to peers focused on work, not person |
| **Shared accountability** | Take responsibility for team outcomes, not just individual contributions |

---

### Professionalism

*Knowing work environments differ greatly, understand and demonstrate effective work habits, and act in the interest of the larger community and workplace.*

| Subskill | Description |
|---|---|
| **Dependability** | Meet deadlines consistently; show up prepared for meetings and commitments |
| **Quality and attention to detail** | Review own work before submission; produce polished, error-free deliverables |
| **Initiative** | Identify next steps without waiting for direction; surface problems early rather than hoping they resolve |
| **Feedback reception** | Receive input without defensiveness; ask questions to understand underlying concerns; incorporate feedback thoughtfully |

---

### Career & Self-Development

*Proactively develop oneself and one's career through continual personal and professional learning, awareness of one's strengths and weaknesses, and navigation of career opportunities.*

| Subskill | Description |
|---|---|
| **Self-awareness** | Recognize own strengths, weaknesses, and areas for growth |
| **Reflection** | Examine own decisions and actions critically; extract lessons from experience |
| **Growth orientation** | Approach challenges as learning opportunities; persist through difficulty |
| **Career articulation** | Connect current experience to future goals; articulate growth and learning to others |

## How Competencies Connect to Assessment

Each competency is assessed through multiple evidence sources. No single deliverable maps to a single competency---rather, rubric dimensions draw on subskills across competencies as appropriate to the work being evaluated.

| Evidence Type | Primary Competencies Assessed |
|---|---|
| **Project Deliverables** | Communication (written clarity), Critical Thinking (all subskills) |
| **Structured Reflections** | Career & Self-Development (all subskills) |
| **Professional Engagement** | Professionalism (all subskills), Communication (professional correspondence, active listening) |
| **Peer Evaluation** | Teamwork (all subskills), Communication (active listening) |
| **Final Presentation** | Communication (oral presentation) |

This multi-source approach ensures that claims about competency development are grounded in observable evidence, not self-report alone. Each evidence type contributes to a complete picture of what students can do.

## A Note on Technology and AI

The NACE framework includes a Technology competency. The Riipen Experience addresses technology use — and AI use in particular — seriously and comprehensively, but through course policy and design rather than as a separately assessed competency.

The competency framework only includes competencies with robust, multi-source assessment evidence. AI conduct and technology use are addressed through:

- An explicit AI Use Policy covering philosophy, permitted and prohibited uses, and week-by-week guidance
- A per-submission AI disclosure requirement on all graded work
- The defend-your-work standard enforced at the Final Presentation, where students must explain and respond to questions about their findings and recommendations in real time
- Rubric criteria across all assessments that reward specificity, evidence, and judgment — qualities that reflect genuine student thinking regardless of what tools were used

This approach treats AI conduct as a professional norm embedded throughout the course rather than a discrete skill assessed in isolation. It is a more operationally substantive treatment than a competency subskill would provide.

## Why This Framework

**Recognized standard.** NACE competencies are the common language of career readiness. Using them means Riipen Experience outcomes connect directly to what employers say they want and what institutions are already tracking.

**Assessable, not aspirational.** Every subskill can be observed in student work. We're not claiming to develop vague qualities like "leadership" or "creativity"---we're developing specific, demonstrable behaviors.

**Consistent across projects.** Whether a student works on a marketing strategy or a data analysis project, the competency framework is the same. This is what makes diverse projects comparable and enables institutional learning over time.

**Design-informed.** The course structure isn't arbitrary---it's built to create conditions where these competencies develop. Employer touchpoints build Communication. Team deliverables build Teamwork. Structured reflections build Career & Self-Development. The framework and the pedagogy reinforce each other.
