# Weekly Activity Breakdown

Week 3 is structured around a single anchor event: the employer kickoff meeting. Everything before it is preparation; everything after it is processing and documentation. The week breaks naturally into two halves, and the module content reflects that split — the AI note-taking primer and video call etiquette module are positioned before the meeting, while the processing, follow-up, and research primers come after. Students arrive in Week 3 having already done organizational reconnaissance in Week 2; the post-kickoff research here is more targeted because scope is now confirmed. The Kickoff Summary is the first team deliverable that requires capturing real information from a real professional interaction rather than completing a planning exercise. Reflection #1 is sequenced at the end of the week — after the kickoff and the Kickoff Summary — so students have a meaningful experience to examine. This week runs over the standard ceiling given the density of the kickoff event and the addition of the first graded reflection. Lighter weeks elsewhere in the course absorb the difference.

# Deliverable Connection

**Kickoff Summary** → Communication: Written clarity

Checkpoint; completion-based. A factual record of the employer's stated expectations from the kickoff meeting — not analysis or interpretation. Must capture confirmed scope, deliverables, success criteria, timeline, key contacts, and open questions. The distinction between what was confirmed and what remains open is the key evaluative criterion for completion. AI-assisted organization of raw notes is permitted and encouraged; AI cannot generate the substance of what was actually discussed. Disclosure required if AI was used.

The Kickoff Summary serves a downstream function: it becomes the evidentiary foundation for the Context Analysis & Project Plan in Week 4. Teams that produce a vague or inaccurate Kickoff Summary will find themselves working against a weak foundation in the first graded deliverable.

---

**Reflection #1 → Career & Self-Development (Reflection, Self-Awareness)**

Graded; rubric-based. Individual submission. Contributes ~5% of the final grade as part of the Reflection Portfolio (25% total, five reflections). Students draw from their Foundation phase experience (Weeks 1–3) — team formation, the kickoff meeting, early research, or any moment of learning or surprise that emerged as they entered the project. The kickoff is the most likely anchor experience given its recency and significance, but students are not limited to it.

This is the first DEAL reflection in the course. The DEAL reference sheet and a pro tip on using AI for reflection are embedded in the assignment prompt to reduce friction on a first attempt. The rubric dimensions are Accurate Description, Critical Examination, Honest Self-Assessment, and Articulation of Learning; expectations are calibrated to a first attempt — depth and honesty matter more than polish. Submitted via the Riipen platform. AI disclosure required.

# AI in This Week

**Permitted and encouraged:**

- Using AI transcription or summarization tools during or immediately after the kickoff meeting to capture what was discussed — introduced in 3.02 before the meeting so students arrive ready
- Using AI to help organize and structure raw notes into the Kickoff Summary format after the meeting
- Using AI to support post-kickoff research — understanding the competitive landscape, sector context, or unfamiliar terminology is an encouraged use case

**Off-limits this week:**

- AI cannot generate the substance of the Kickoff Summary — the content must reflect what was actually said in the meeting; AI-generated summaries that were not grounded in a real conversation are a disclosure violation
- The reflection (3.18) must be grounded in the student's own experience — AI cannot supply the experience, the examination, or the articulated learning; the pro tip embedded in the reflection prompt explains how to use AI appropriately as a thinking partner rather than a content generator

**Disclosure required** on the Kickoff Summary if AI was used in organizing or drafting. Disclosure also required on Reflection #1 — if AI was not used, a one-sentence statement to that effect is sufficient.

**Prompt strategy in use this week:** Give AI enough context — when using AI to organize meeting notes, students should share the raw notes, the project brief, and the Kickoff Summary format before asking AI to help structure the output. Introduced in Week 2; applied in context here for the first time.
