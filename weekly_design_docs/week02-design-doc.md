## Weekly Activity Breakdown

Week 2 is anchored by team formation. Teams have just been assigned, and students are meeting each other for the first time while simultaneously taking on a real employer project. The module opens with Using AI Responsibly so that students have a shared frame for AI use before their first team meeting and charter conversation. The week's job is to get the team functional — a shared charter, a first meeting, and an outreach email sent — while beginning the background research that will carry into Weeks 3 and 4. The week runs at 9 hrs 20 min by design: team formation involves inherent inefficiency and coordination overhead that cannot be productively filled.

## Deliverable Connection

**Team Charter** → Teamwork: Shared accountability

Checkpoint; completion-based. The charter establishes operating agreements the team negotiates itself: communication norms, contribution expectations, decision-making process, and how conflict will be handled. The Setting Your Team Up for Success module gives students a practical framework for that conversation before they sit down together. AI can assist with formatting or refining language after the team has agreed on the substance, but the substance must come from the team's own discussion — the charter is a relational document, not a drafting exercise. The first team meeting itself should not involve AI for the same reason.

**Employer Outreach Email** → Communication: Professional correspondence

Checkpoint; completion-based. The team's first professional correspondence with the employer contact. Must introduce the team, demonstrate awareness of the project, and request the kickoff meeting. All team members must agree on the final version before it is sent. Students have email basics from Week 1 and are applying a known skill in a higher-stakes context rather than learning it fresh. AI-assisted drafting is permitted; disclosure required if used.

## AI in This Week

**Permitted and encouraged:**

- Using AI to help draft the employer outreach email — drafting with AI assistance and then reviewing, personalizing, and agreeing on it as a team is appropriate professional practice; disclosure required
- Using AI to help with initial organizational research — understanding the domain, sector, and organization's context is an encouraged use case
- Using AI to brainstorm questions for the kickoff meeting

**Off-limits this week:**

- The Team Charter must be negotiated and drafted by the team — the substance must come from the team's own discussion; AI can help format or refine language after the team has agreed, but cannot generate the relational agreements the charter is meant to capture
- The first team meeting itself should not involve AI — this is relationship-building time

**Disclosure required** on both checkpoints (Team Charter and Employer Outreach Email) if AI was used in drafting.

**Prompt strategy introduced:** How to use AI as a thinking partner. Three principles introduced in the Using AI Responsibly module:

- Give AI enough context before asking a question — share the project brief, your role, and what you're trying to accomplish
- Ask AI to generate options, not answers — "what are some ways we could approach X" is more useful than "what should we do about X"
- Always ask: what am I assuming here?
