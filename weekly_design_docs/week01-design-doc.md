# Weekly Activity Breakdown

Week 1 is weighted toward orientation and exploration rather than project production — deliberately so. Teams have not yet been assigned, so no collaborative project work is possible. The week's job is to establish the learning frame, surface students' starting points, and build anticipatory readiness for team formation in Week 2. The module content introduces what experiential learning is, what working in an AI-enabled professional context requires, and what the course will ask of students before they have a team or a project to anchor those ideas to.

# Deliverable Connection

**Student Intake Questionnaire** → Career & Self-Development: Self-awareness

Checkpoint; completion-based. Frames the purpose of the intake questionnaire, how it will support project matching, and how it should be used for reflection and goal-tracking throughout the course. Must be completed without AI assistance; responses feed personalized reflection prompts later in the course, and AI-generated answers break that personalization chain. Not graded for quality, but honest completion is foundational to how the reflection system works. Students who approach this as a compliance task rather than a genuine self-assessment will get less out of the reflection sequence in Weeks 3, 6, 9, 12, and 15.

**Preference Submission** → Critical Thinking: Prioritization

Platform task, not a standalone graded deliverable. The mechanism through which team and project assignments are made — students who do not complete it may be assigned by default. The written justification requirement makes this more than an administrative step; it develops reasoned decision-making and surfaces student priorities, which is the first instance of evidence-based reasoning in the course.

# AI in This Week

**Permitted and encouraged:**

- Using AI to compare and evaluate project briefs — generating summary questions or a comparison framework to evaluate options across multiple briefs is a legitimate use of AI as a thinking tool
- Using AI to draft the LMS introduction post, then personalizing it before posting

**Off-limits this week:**

- The Student Intake Questionnaire must be completed without AI assistance — this is one of the few hard prohibitions in the course; it is explicitly stated in the module and the assignment instructions; AI-generated responses break the personalization chain that the reflection system depends on

**No disclosure required this week** — no graded work is submitted, so the disclosure requirement is not yet in effect.

**Policy context:** The AI in Today's Workplace & This Course module (1.04) introduces the full policy. Students are expected to have read it before Week 2, when graded work and AI-assisted tasks begin. No specific prompt strategies are introduced this week — that begins in Week 2 with the Using AI Responsibly module. The AI-as-Socratic-interlocutor strategy for DEAL reflections is introduced in Week 2 and first applied in Week 3.
